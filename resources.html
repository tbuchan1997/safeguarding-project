Additional Resources and Support

Here are UK-based resources categorized according to specific safeguarding concerns. These organizations and helplines provide valuable support for various types of abuse and safeguarding issues.

Resources for Safeguarding Children

NSPCC (National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children)

The NSPCC works to prevent child abuse and protect children at risk. They offer advice and support to anyone concerned about a child's welfare.

Helpline: 0808 800 5000 (available 24/7)

Website: www.nspcc.org.uk

Resources for Safeguarding Adults and the Elderly

Action on Elder Abuse (Hourglass)

Hourglass addresses abuse and neglect of older adults and provides support to those concerned about elder abuse.

Helpline: 0808 808 8141

Website: www.wearehourglass.org

Resources for Financial Abuse

Action Fraud

The UK's national reporting center for fraud and cybercrime. You can report instances of financial abuse, scams, or fraud targeting vulnerable individuals.

Helpline: 0300 123 2040

Website: www.actionfraud.police.uk

Mental Health and Emotional Support

Mind (Mental Health Charity)

Offers support for individuals experiencing mental health issues, including those arising from abuse or safeguarding concerns.

Helpline: 0300 123 3393

Website: www.mind.org.uk

Support for Whistleblowers

Protect (formerly Public Concern at Work)

Protect is a whistleblowing charity that offers advice on raising concerns about malpractice, including safeguarding violations.

Helpline: 020 3117 2520

Website: www.protect-advice.org.uk

General Safeguarding Resources

Social Care Institute for Excellence (SCIE)

Offers training, resources, and consultancy services on safeguarding for adults and children. They help organizations improve their safeguarding practices.

Website: www.scie.org.uk
